# V4-C Redesign Candidate Matrix

None of these candidates is implemented by this task. Each is a bounded module-level proposal and must preserve the existing CBF-QP and non-planner boundary.

| ID | Mechanism and hypothesis | Difference from original | Expected benefit / runtime effect | Required interface and risks | Minimum prototype | Success, failure, and stop rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| R-V4C-1 | **Hierarchical Candidate Evaluation.** Deterministic families often suffice; defer random/CEM until they do not produce a feasible sequence. | Original evaluates all enabled families together. | Fewer H-step GSplat queries when deterministic candidates pass; possible loss if random is uniquely needed. | Needs family partitions and deterministic feasibility gate; no CBF-QP change. Selection changes only when second stage is skipped. | Stage A: baseline, braking, repulsive, goal-directed, continuity. Stage B: original random/CEM only if Stage A has no feasible candidate. | Success: no safety/feasibility regression versus original on fixed activated cases and at least 20% lower activated-step median recovery runtime. Failure: any original-feasible context becomes infeasible, or runtime target is missed. Stop on either condition. |
| R-V4C-2 | **Candidate-Family Adaptive Budget.** Fixed rules allocate bounded samples by warning type, velocity, critical geometry, and prior recovery result. | Original uses a uniform random count. | Spend samples on relevant families; may lower runtime or increase useful diversity. | Requires compact warning/geometry feature contract; risk of brittle hand rules and family starvation. No learned policy. | Fixed lookup table with per-family caps summing to original maximum budget. | Success: equal local feasibility with lower candidate count on pre-registered contexts. Failure: any cap removes a unique feasible family or no runtime reduction. Stop rather than add learned adaptation. |
| R-V4C-3 | **Persistent-Warning Recovery Memory.** Remember selected family/control class, post-recovery verification, and recurrence to avoid repeating ineffective recovery. | Original is step-local apart from `u_prev`. | Reduces repeated ineffective activation; may improve diagnosis of unresolved route risk. | Requires bounded per-trial memory and context key; risk of stale memory and implicit planning. | One-step suppression of immediately repeated family after failed post-recovery verification; never suppress baseline safety check. | Success: reduced repeat failure/recurrence with no safety regression. Failure: suppression removes later feasible recovery. Stop and keep memory diagnostic-only. |
| R-V4C-4 | **Safety-Progress Pareto Recovery Selection.** Prefer verified progress-nonworse feasible candidates, then explicit tradeoff candidates, before unverified candidates. | Original uses one weighted scalar cost. | Makes progress tradeoff visible and reduces weight dependence. | Needs common-horizon progress metric and tie-break contract; risk of objective redesign and planner-boundary creep. | Label feasible candidates as progress-nonworse, progress-tradeoff, or unverified, then retain original cost only within a label. | Success: no local safety regression and lower selected progress regret. Failure: labels are unstable or do not improve regret. Stop rather than tune weights repeatedly. |
| R-V4C-5 | **Trigger-Severity-Aware Recovery.** Use H1/H2/H3 severity to choose no search, deterministic search, or full search. | Original uses all-or-nothing activation from one horizon minimum. | Avoid full search for mild warning; may reduce activated runtime. | Requires severity contract and post-recovery verifier; risk of late intervention. No supervisor or halt semantics. | Fixed clear/warning/margin-violation/critical bins mapped to no, deterministic-only, or full original search. | Success: no pre-registered H-step regression with reduced full-search count. Failure: a downgraded context loses original feasibility. Stop at first safety regression. |

## Comparison

R-V4C-1 directly addresses supported runtime concentration without changing CBF-QP or inventing a new objective. R-V4C-5 is a complementary activation mechanism. R-V4C-2 requires stronger feature/cap evidence; R-V4C-3 requires recurrence telemetry; and R-V4C-4 requires Pareto evidence before changing selection. None is a planner, learned policy, or safety proof.

## R-V4C-1 Status

Frozen hierarchical V0 is retained for the validated flight H3_N128 configuration only. Its held-out audit met the feasibility, safety, runtime, progress, and nonactivated-path guardrails, but Stage B was exercised only on contexts that remained failed under the original full search. No candidate-family removal, parameter tuning, broader benchmark, or R1 work is authorized by this result.
